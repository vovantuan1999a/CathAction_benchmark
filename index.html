Abtracts

We proposed the CathAction Dataset, a benchmark for Endovascular Catheterization understanding. We proposed an architecture method that combines an Efficient Transformer-based on an efficient local spatial-temporal separation attention mechanism and DETR-Object Detection with Transformers for catheter action anticipation and collision localization task. Besides, to encourage research into endovascular catheterization action in more realistic scenarios, we have built an Endovascular Catheterization action dataset consisting of well-defined endovascular catheterization action annotations and bounding-box collision between endovascular surgical equipment and vessel-walls.


Overview

CathAction is a benchmark dataset for the Endovascular Catheterization understanding task. The features of CathAction includes

  • A total of 569 videos, consists of 2,523 action segments that were extracted from 569 lengthy videos. Videos that did not contain any events or actions were excluded from the dataset.
  • We annotate a total of 54, 158 bounding boxes were collected for normal or collision action classes, and both collision-class and normal-class bounding boxes were used in reporting results for the collision localization challenge..
  • The training subset includes $2,144$ action segments and their related bounding boxes, while the testing subset contains $379$ action segments and their related bounding boxes.
  • The CathAction dataset includes data on the different actions taken during endovascular catheterization procedures, with each action being classified into one of five categories: rotate, advance guidewire, retract catheter, retract guidewire, and advance catheter..


Download

You can download the CathAction dataset from here.

The folder is in the following architecture:

CathAction-datasets/
├── annotations
│   ├── training_clip.csv
│   ├── validation_clip.csv
└── rgb-frames
│   ├── frames.zip
└── labels-collision-boxes
    ├── labels.zip

where *.csv store the meta-data, *.txt are annotation labels-collision-boxes, and *.zip contain rgb-frames.
